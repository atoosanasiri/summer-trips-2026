Backus Woods — 15+ km Loop
Port Rowan, Norfolk County · Six trails · 21 km total network
Canada's largest remaining old-growth Carolinian forest. Six public trails covering 21 km total — string together 3–4 loops to hit your 15+ km target. Individual trails run 2.3–6.5 km each. The forest is extraordinary: Tulip Trees shooting 35 metres through the canopy, Black Gums that were seedlings when Cartier first sailed into the Gulf of St. Lawrence, Jefferson Salamanders, Prothonotary Warblers. Bring water and bug spray — it's lush, humid, and alive in July. Allow 4–5 hrs for 15+ km.
21 km trail network String 3–4 loops Free · No pass needed 4–5 hrs Bug spray essential

Backus
Woods

One of the oldest forests in Canada and the finest remaining example of Carolinian old-growth in the country. Six trails, 21 km of network, ancient trees, and species found nowhere else in Canada.

Six public trails totalling 21 km — combine 3–4 for your 15+ km target
Black Gum trees over 400 years old — seedlings when Europeans first explored Canada
Tulip Trees reaching 35 metres — tallest trees east of the Rockies
25% of Canada's species at risk found in Carolinian zone — many in Backus Woods
Free and open year-round — no reservation needed
Trail map: norfolkcounty.ca/Backus_Woods_Trail.pdf
"The Carolinian Life Zone covers less than 1% of Canada's land — yet holds 25% of its endangered species. Backus Woods is its crown jewel."

Turkey Point Beach — Kayak & SUP
Turkey Point Provincial Park, Norfolk County · ~25 min from cabin
Two kilometres of sandy Lake Erie beach inside Turkey Point Provincial Park. Waveline Rentals on-site rents kayaks, paddleboards, canoes, and jet boats — no need to bring your own unless you prefer it. The Long Point Bay paddling area is sheltered and calm, with the UNESCO Biosphere Reserve stretching into the distance. Paddle toward Long Point for open-water Erie views, or explore the creek inlets for wildlife. Allow 3–4 hrs on the water.

🛍 Farmers Market
Brantford Farmers' Market
Brantford · Open Saturdays 7AM–2PM year-round · ~1 hr from cabin
One of Ontario's oldest markets — established in 1848 and still running every Saturday. 49 indoor stalls plus 15–20 outdoor vendors in summer: fresh produce, meats, deli, dairy, baked goods, prepared food, crafts. It's on your route home and it's the real thing — not a curated artisan market, just a proper working community market. Arrive before 2 PM closing.
Est. 1848 Sat 7AM–2PM 49+ stalls On route home

Paris, Ontario — Optional Stop
Paris, ON · 15 min east of Brantford · On Hwy 403
One of Ontario's most underrated small towns — cobblestone streets, stone buildings on the Grand River, good independent coffee shops and lunch spots. 30–45 minutes here is effortless. The downtown is compact and walkable, right on the river. Then it's a straight 90-minute run back to Toronto on the 403.

What to Pack
Bring your own sheets (twin bunk above, double below) and towels — not provided without a fee. Bug spray is mandatory for Backus Woods in July. Water shoes for the rocky Lake Erie shore. Cash for firewood ($15/bundle). No glass bottles at the cabin.

Kayak Rentals
Waveline Rentals at Turkey Point is on-site — no booking needed but July weekends can get busy, arrive early. Backup: Norfolk Kayak & SUP Rentals (NKS) in Simcoe — call ahead. If all else fails, your own SUP on Turkey Point beach is perfect.

Fire Pit Rules
The fire pit at the bunkie requires pre-arrangement with hosts Erik & Angela and is subject to township fire regulations. Never toss a cigarette on the ground or in the outhouse. Quiet hours are 10PM–8AM — there are other campers nearby in the forest campground.
