Day 01 · Friday

Algonquin First

Leave Toronto at 6 AM before the city wakes up. Hit Algonquin by mid-morning for a full trail day before checking in to the cabin.

Roads are clear at 6 AM. No 400-series traffic, no construction crawl. Pack a thermos and drive straight through, or stop in Barrie (~45 min) for coffee and gas. You're at Algonquin's west gate by 8:30 AM — early enough to beat the parking crunch.

Algonquin West Gate
Hwy 60, Algonquin Provincial Park
Buy your day pass at the west gate (~$20/vehicle — buy online in advance on summer weekends, they sell out). The Hwy 60 corridor gives you access to multiple trails without driving deep into the park. Pick up a trail map at the visitor centre.

Centennial Ridges Trail
Algonquin Provincial Park · 10 km loop · 4–5 hrs
The best trail in Algonquin. Exposed Canadian Shield ridgeline, panoramic views over a sea of boreal forest, and a summit that earns its sweat. The 10 km loop takes 4–5 hours at a comfortable pace — bring water, snacks, and bug spray. This is the one trail in Algonquin that requires no asterisk.
10 km loop 4–5 hrs Exposed ridgeline Best views in Algonquin

Post-hike swim in Shield lake water — cold, clear, and surrounded by white pines. Tea Lake and Canoe Lake are both accessible off Hwy 60 with day-use areas. 30–45 minutes here before heading to the cabin.
Post-hike swim Day use Bring towel

Saturday Highlight · Side Trip
Skeleton
Lake

Formed by a meteor strike 800 million years ago, Skeleton Lake is one of Muskoka's most dramatic swimming spots. Anderson's Island has a cliff-jumping area on its south-east side with overhanging granite dropping into crystal-clear, deep water.

Located ~17 km west of Huntsville on Hwy 141 — 30 min from the cabin
Anderson's Island is public-use — reach it by kayak or canoe from Skeleton Lake Marina boat launch
Lake depth up to 65 metres — meteor crater origin, crystal-clear water
Devil's Face Cliff visible on the shoreline from the water
Kayak rentals available in Huntsville — book ahead for Saturday

Rotary Waterfront Trail
Parry Sound · Georgian Bay · 6 km · ~1.5 hrs
Drive to Parry Sound (~40 min) while the morning is still quiet. The Rotary & Algonquin Regiment Fitness Trail hugs the Georgian Bay shoreline for 6 km along pink granite outcroppings — one of the best easy waterfront walks in the region. Views of the 30,000 Islands, calm water, no crowds at 7 AM. Done by 9:00 AM with a proper appetite.

What to Pack
Hiking boots for Algonquin, swim gear + dry bag for Skeleton Lake, bug spray (July in Muskoka is serious), a dry layer for the evening, and water shoes for the rocky cliff area. No pets allowed at the cabin.
🏄
Book SUP in Advance
White Squall Paddling Centre (north of Parry Sound, 705-203-2955) or Killbear Park Mall (705-771-9646) both rent SUP boards. July Sundays fill up — call ahead. White Squall requires 2-day minimum; Killbear is 24-hr rental.
🚦
Skeleton Lake Logistics
Anderson's Island is accessible only by water. Kayak rentals are available in Huntsville — book ahead for Saturday. The public boat launch is at Skeleton Lake Marina on Hwy 141. Cell service is limited around the lake.
