Lion's Head Trail
Lion's Head Provincial Park · Bruce Peninsula
A gem of the Bruce Trail — dramatic limestone cliffs drop straight into Georgian Bay. The loop to the headland is ~7 km with panoramic views that stop you cold. Expect some scrambling, exposed ridgeline, and one of the finest lookouts in Ontario. Allow 2.5–3 hrs.

Grotto + Little Cove Loop
Bruce Peninsula National Park · ~8 km · under 6 hrs
The Grotto is one of Canada's most photographed natural wonders — a sea cave carved into the Niagara Escarpment where turquoise Georgian Bay water glows an impossible blue. The loop continues to Little Cove, a hidden pebble beach perfect for a swim. The full trail is ~8 km on rugged terrain; expect scrambling over boulders and roots. Absolutely worth every step.
~8 km loop Under 6 hrs Sea cave Swim stop Rocky terrain

What to Pack
Hiking boots (the Grotto trail is rocky), swim gear and a dry bag, bug spray (the peninsula is serious in June), layers for evening fire, and a headlamp for late-night outhouse trips.
🚗
Parking & Parks
Bruce Peninsula National Park requires a day-use pass ($12/vehicle). The Halfway Log Dump lot fills by 9 AM on summer weekends — arrive early or you'll park on Cyprus Lake Road and add 2 km to your walk.
📡
Cell Service
Spotty north of Owen Sound. Download offline maps before you leave Toronto. The Grotto trail is well-marked but having AllTrails offline saves anxiety. Let someone know your itinerary.
