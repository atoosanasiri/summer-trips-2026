Morning Hike — Lion's Head or Blue Mountain
Bruce Peninsula · Choose your trail
Option A — Lion's Head Trail (~7 km loop, 2.5–3 hrs): Dramatic limestone cliffs dropping straight into Georgian Bay. Exposed ridgeline, panoramic views, some scrambling. One of the finest lookouts in Ontario — worth every step.

Option B — Blue Mountain, Collingwood (~varies): A slightly earlier stop on the way north with ski-hill trails and Georgian Bay views. Good choice if you want a shorter hike before a long drive day.

Either way — eat breakfast up on the trail with a view. Sandwich, granola, whatever you packed. That's the plan.

Halfway Log Dump to The Grotto
Bruce Peninsula National Park · 6 km return · Challenging
One of Canada's most spectacular short hikes. The 6 km return trail from Halfway Log Dump leads to the Grotto — a sea cave carved into the Niagara Escarpment where Georgian Bay water glows an impossible shade of turquoise. Expect rugged terrain, limestone scrambling, exposed rock faces, and at least one moment where you stop and just stare. Challenging but absolutely worth it. Allow 2–3 hours on the trail.

Choose Your Morning Hike
Wiarton & Bruce Peninsula · All trailheads within 20 min
Four good options — pick based on energy, time, and mood. All are within easy reach of Wiarton. Pack snacks and eat on the trail.
Option 1 Hope Bay Forest
~5.6 km loop 2–3 hrs Moderate Free entry

A rewarding loop through Hope Bay Forest Provincial Nature Reserve with cliff-edge views over Hope Bay and Lake Huron. Rocky and rooty underfoot — proper trail shoes needed. Not technical, but it demands attention. 4.7 stars on AllTrails from 130+ reviews.

🐛 Tick territory — tuck socks into pants, check after
Option 2 Spirit Rock Conservation Area
~5 km trails Easy–Moderate $10 entry Cash only

A local gem just outside Wiarton. Hike the escarpment trails overlooking Colpoy's Bay and explore the atmospheric ruins of the McNeill Estate — a 17-room stone mansion from the late 1800s, slowly being reclaimed by the forest. Then descend the famous spiral staircase (3 tiers, 30 mesh steps) down the cliffside to the water. Small parking lot (12–15 cars), so arrive early on a summer Sunday.

💵 $10 entry — cash only, bring bills 🔦 Bring a flashlight for the cave sections
Option 3 The Spiral Staircase (Spirit Rock)
Short & sweet ~1 hr Easy Same site as Option 2

If you want something shorter, skip the full Spirit Rock loop and head straight for the spiral staircase — the standout feature of the conservation area. Three tiers of mesh steps wind down the Niagara Escarpment face to the turquoise water of Colpoy's Bay below. Unique, photogenic, and surprisingly fun. Same entry fee applies.

💵 $10 entry — cash only 📷 Best photo stop of the whole weekend
Option 4 Skinner's Bluff Trail
~10 km loop 2.5–3 hrs Moderate Free

Walk the long escarpment edge at Skinner's Bluff for 180-degree views over Georgian Bay and its islands — Griffith, White Cloud, and Hay. Rocky footpath with roots and crevices, but nothing technical. Often combined with Bruce's Caves nearby for a bonus underground detour. One of the most scenic moderate hikes in Grey County.

🏔 Combine with Bruce's Caves for an extra 30 min adventure
