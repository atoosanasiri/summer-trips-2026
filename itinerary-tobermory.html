Burnt Point Loop & Little Dunks Lookout
Bruce Peninsula National Park · Near Tobermory NP Visitor Centre
The golden hour stop. Burnt Point Loop is a 5.3 km moderate trail through forest to multiple cliff-edge viewpoints over Georgian Bay — including a lookout tower at the start with a full 360° panorama of Tobermory and the bay. The loop also passes Little Dunks Bay Lookout, a short spur to a viewing platform with Parks Canada Red Chairs right over the turquoise water — one of the best sunset perches on the peninsula. Sunset in late June is around 9 PM, so the light during this hike is extraordinary.

If energy is low, skip the full loop and just do the Little Dunks Lookout alone — 800 m return, 25 minutes, almost flat, with the same jaw-dropping view.
5.3 km loop · ~1h20 full OR 800 m · 25 min quick Lookout tower Golden hour Sunset ~9PM

Halfway Log Dump to The Grotto
Bruce Peninsula National Park · 6 km return · Challenging
One of Canada’s most spectacular short hikes. The 6 km return trail leads to the Grotto — a sea cave carved into the Niagara Escarpment where Georgian Bay water glows turquoise. Expect limestone scrambling, exposed rock faces, and rugged terrain throughout. Challenging but absolutely worth it. Allow 2–3 hours.

Choose Your Morning Hike
Wiarton & Bruce Peninsula · All trailheads within 20 min
Four good options — pick based on energy, time, and mood. Pack snacks and eat on the trail.
Option 1 Hope Bay Forest
~5.6 km loop 2–3 hrs Moderate Free entry

A rewarding loop through Hope Bay Forest Provincial Nature Reserve with cliff-edge views over Hope Bay and Lake Huron. Rocky and rooty underfoot — proper trail shoes needed. 4.7 stars on AllTrails from 130+ reviews.

🐛 Tick territory — tuck socks into pants, check after
Option 2 Spirit Rock Conservation Area
~5 km trails Easy–Moderate $10 entry Cash only

A local gem just outside Wiarton. Hike the escarpment trails over Colpoy’s Bay and explore the ruins of the McNeill Estate — a 17-room stone mansion from the late 1800s slowly being reclaimed by forest. Then descend the famous spiral staircase (3 tiers, 30 mesh steps) down the cliffside to the water. Small parking lot (12–15 cars) — arrive early.

💵 $10 entry — cash only, bring bills 🔦 Bring a flashlight for the cave sections
Option 3 The Spiral Staircase (Spirit Rock)
Short & sweet ~1 hr Easy Same site as Option 2

Skip the full loop and head straight for the staircase — the standout feature of Spirit Rock. Three tiers of mesh steps wind down the Niagara Escarpment face to the turquoise water of Colpoy’s Bay below. Unique, photogenic, and surprisingly fun. Same entry fee applies.

💵 $10 entry — cash only 📷 Best photo stop of the whole weekend
Option 4 Skinner’s Bluff Trail
~10 km loop 2.5–3 hrs Moderate Free

Walk the long escarpment edge at Skinner’s Bluff for 180-degree views over Georgian Bay and its islands — Griffith, White Cloud, and Hay. Rocky footpath with roots and crevices but nothing technical. Often combined with Bruce’s Caves nearby for a bonus underground detour. One of the most scenic moderate hikes in Grey County.

🏔 Combine with Bruce’s Caves for an extra 30 min underground adventure
