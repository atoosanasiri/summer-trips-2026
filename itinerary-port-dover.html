🛍 Silver Lake Market
Silver Lake Farmers' Market
320 St. Patrick St, Port Dover · Sat 8AM–1PM · Every week
Right in Port Dover — no detour needed. Run by the Port Dover Lions Club in a 22,000 sq ft building on the banks of Silver Lake. Fresh vegetables, fruits, meats, fish, cheese, and baked goods from 15+ vendors. Walt's Place Kitchen inside serves a hot breakfast with free coffee or tea — perfect way to start the morning before heading to the beach. Pick up anything you need for tonight's BBQ.
8AM–1PM Free coffee Hot breakfast inside 15+ vendors BBQ supplies

Harbour Brunch
Port Dover Harbour · Main Street
Port Dover's harbour is one of the most charming on Lake Erie — fishing boats, the lighthouse, and a strip of waterfront restaurants. Grab a proper brunch at one of the harbour spots before the water. The town is walkable and compact — take your time before loading up for the afternoon.
Harbour views Port Dover Lighthouse Walkable downtown

SUP on Lake Erie — Port Dover Beach
Port Dover Beach · Lake Erie
Port Dover beach sits right on Lake Erie — sandy, wide, and usually warm in late July. Bring your own SUP or contact Norfolk Kayak & SUP Rentals (NKS) who specifically service Port Dover as one of their paddle sites. The harbour inlet is sheltered for launching; once out on the open lake the views are spectacular. Paddle along the shoreline toward the pier or head east toward Turkey Point. Allow 2–3 hrs.
Bring your SUP NKS Rentals (Simcoe) Harbour launch 2–3 hrs Lake Erie open water

Option A · Bike
Lynn Valley Trail — Cycle
The best rail trail in Norfolk County — 10 km each way from Port Dover to Simcoe, 20 km return. Flat gravel surface on an old rail bed through shaded woodlands, four trestle bridges, past ginseng fields and quiet farmland. 95% shaded so perfect for a warm July morning. Leave at 7 AM, back by 10 AM, checkout by 11 AM, on the road home by noon. Bike rentals available locally if needed.
20 km return Flat gravel 95% shaded ~2.5 hrs 4 trestle bridges
🦶
Option B · Hike
Lynn Valley Trail — Walk
Same trail on foot — do the full 10 km one way or a 6–8 km out-and-back from Port Dover. The trail follows the Lynn River through deep shady woods, with benches along the way and no real elevation. Ideal for a slow Sunday morning walk before a long drive. Start at Silver Lake (east end of the trail in Port Dover) and go as far as you feel like, then turn back. Done by 10 AM.
6–10 km Flat + shaded Lynn River Flexible distance
